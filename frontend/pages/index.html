💧

FAQ

Common questions

Everything you need to know before running your first assessment.

Is GlucoSense a replacement for a doctor?
No. GlucoSense is a screening and educational tool. It surfaces risk based on your inputs but does not diagnose, prescribe, or treat any condition. Always consult a qualified healthcare professional for medical decisions.
Do I need lab results to use it?
Yes, for the most accurate result you'll want values from a recent blood panel — fasting glucose, creatinine, haemoglobin, etc. If you don't have exact numbers, you can still use approximate ranges; the model handles missing or near-zero values via median imputation.
How accurate are the models?
Our diabetes model (Logistic Regression on Pima Indians, 768 samples) achieves a ROC-AUC of roughly 0.83 on held-out test data. Our CKD model (Random Forest on UCI CKD, 400 samples) achieves ROC-AUC ≈ 0.99. Real-world accuracy will always depend on your input quality.
Is my health data private?
Your data is stored in an account protected by JWT-based authentication and bcrypt-hashed passwords. Assessments are only visible to you, and we do not share your records with any third party.
Can I use it for patients in my clinic?
GlucoSense is intended for educational and research use. It has not been approved as a regulated medical device. Clinicians using it as a discussion aid should always verify results against established clinical guidelines.
